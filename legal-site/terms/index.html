6 Mandatory Human Review Process REQUIRED

This section is foundational to the service. CreatorDraft is designed as a creator-assistive tool, not an autonomous posting system. Every video must pass through your personal review before any upload occurs.

6.1 Review Before Every Upload

Every video generated by CreatorDraft must be individually reviewed and explicitly approved by you — the connected creator — before it is uploaded to TikTok. This is enforced at the system level; no video can be transmitted to TikTok's API without a recorded approval action from a logged-in user.

6.2 What Review Means

Your approval constitutes your confirmation that you have:

  • Watched the generated video in full within the CreatorDraft dashboard
  • Reviewed and edited the caption and hashtags to your satisfaction
  • Determined the content complies with TikTok's Community Guidelines and your niche standards
  • Determined the content complies with TikTok Shop affiliate program disclosure requirements
  • Authorized the video to be uploaded to your TikTok draft inbox

6.3 No Auto-Publishing

CreatorDraft uploads content exclusively to your TikTok draft inbox — not to your public profile. After upload, the video sits as an unposted draft. Publishing the draft to your public TikTok feed requires a separate, manual action by you inside:

  • The TikTok mobile app (Profile → Drafts → select draft → Post), or
  • TikTok Creator Center

CreatorDraft has no mechanism to trigger public posting and does not interact with the TikTok video.publish endpoint.

By approving a draft for upload, you accept full responsibility for that content. Your approval action is a legally meaningful act of authorization. CreatorDraft is a tool; you are the author and publisher of record for all content you approve and later publish on TikTok.

CreatorDraft enforces a configurable maximum of 3 draft uploads per day per connected TikTok account. This limit exists to prevent abuse and to keep usage within patterns consistent with individual creator activity. Higher limits require written authorization from us.
